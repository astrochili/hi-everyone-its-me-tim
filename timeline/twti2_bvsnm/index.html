Don’t assume that players will go somewhere first or talk to someone first. What this means is — I wanted to remind level designers that just because you put a guard outside the town, don’t assume that everybody will stop and talk to that guard. If you want to force that, you can put a gate in the town that doesn’t open until you talk to the guard, or worse, force the guard to go into conversation with the player. And you may think: well, at that point it’s okay, because everyone in the town will attack you.

In general, I just like to remind level designers: don’t assume anything the player is going to do in a particular order. Your job is to provide a nice map for them to play on and not make such assumptions. So, number two, remember that all of our playthrough builds — and there’s a link to where I listed all the different major types — Remember that all of our playthrough builds must be supported on the main story quests, all of them.
