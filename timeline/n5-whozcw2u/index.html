One: a game can mean different things to different people. So when I see people talking about what things mean in Fallout, none of you are wrong. We can talk about what I intended or what another one of the designers intended or what Bethesda now intends or whatever, but at the end of the day, the meaning is the meaning that you brought to it, because nobody plays the game quite like you do. Nobody therefore has the same experience that you do, and that’s a huge way that games are different than books and movies.

That is not true in a lot of games. It’s certainly not true in any of my games. That’s why I like non-linear games, but because of that, not any different sequence of events, but different events themselves can happen. I think that the meaning of a game is highly dependent on the player. So that has a corollary that I don’t think people think about a lot, which means no one can force meaning into a game — uh, not, you, not any of your friends, not any of people online.

Not any of the reviewers and certainly not the creators. The only meaning in that game is the one that was created by you playing it. And I think all those different groups I mentioned will have very different experiences and backgrounds that they bring to the game, and so games can just mean wildly different things to them. And I think this whole argument about whether games or art is kind of silly. I can see why people may have thought that 20 years ago. I still think they’re wrong, but I can see, in the era of Atari and Nintendo games, people going: yeah, I don’t think these are, I think they’re wrong. But I don’t see how anyone can look at modern games and go: these are not works of art, they’re beautiful, they’re well crafted, there’s a lot of time and thought put into them. I think that some games 50 or 100 years now, years from now, are really going to be held up as works of art. And, like all art, the game itself isn’t the — and I’m going to quote Buddhism here — the game isn’t the path to meaning. The game points to the path and it’s up to you whether you want to take that path.
