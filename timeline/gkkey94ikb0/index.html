Let me talk about first about what went right during my development of this little toy. First of all, dice physics. That was why I did it I wanted to model some six-sided dice in all of their physics glory, because I thought that would be fun. In other words, I wanted to get the bounce right: how far, how fast they fall, how much they bounce when they hit the surface, the friction when they hit the surface, the, physics of detecting what side is pointing up, In other words, when the dice stops moving, detecting what face is up. And I did that, by the way, by shooting a vector on the other side down and detecting that that intersected the table’s collision box.

And what that? What meant was that there was a vector off each face, an existing vector. I could label that vector, and that vector was labeled with the other side’s number. So the vector pointing down on the opposite side of the four was four, The one on the opposite side of one was one. So if the one vector intersected the table when the dice came to a halt, I knew the.

One was facing up, Made it very easy, Loved it. There were some edge cases, so even a very, very simple toy like this immediately started doing edge cases And what happened is sometimes the dice would roll and one would lean against another like this. So it wasn’t, there was no face. That was up the vector, because I made the vector really short, because you don’t, I don’t want it, if it’s leaning against a die, to be so far that it intersects the table anyway. So it’s a very short little vector coming out the bottom.

So when they leaned like this, they it, they. I knew that their speed was zero but the vector didn’t intersect anything. What I did to overcome that was, as soon as the speed hit zero and there was no intersecting vector, I gave it a random force nudge.

What was cool? So so it moved again, and usually rolled off and intersected, came to a stop with an intersecting vector. If it didn’t, I gave it another force nudge. What was great about this is you can’t see it in the resulting game because when it comes to a stop it I can detect that stop instantly and give it the force nudge. So what you see is boom, You didn’t see that it came to a stop because it instantly got that nudge.

But meanwhile every time you had to roll to get dice you took a little bit of damage. So it was like a combat: You’d roll to hit and meanwhile you were getting hit by the monster. You could do damage to the monster by putting some dice on the card. Once you got all the dice on the card and that card went away, monster defeated. That was a good basic game design that I had in my toy.
