Hi everyone. It’s me, Tim. Today I want to talk about RPG myths, and what do I mean by that?

I’m talking about lies that RPGs try to sell you, in other words, things that many — and I would even argue most — role playing games have in them, either as mechanics or just the way the story unfolds. That is not true in real life, just absolute fabrication.

Let’s start with little ones and I’ll work my way up to the big ones. Probably one of the ones that I see frequently in games that I always smile because I I see it a lot. I’m just like: oh, there it is again — is that when your character crouches, their gun accuracy goes up.

I’ve looked this up across the decades. I’ve tried to look up to see if this is true, and it’s.

Let’s just say that people have varying opinions: gun experts, the military — there’s various opinions on this about whether or not crouching down would increase your gun accuracy. There’s stuff about points of contact, and whether you’re leaning against something and the surface you’re crouching on, and whether you’re down just squatting or whether you actually have one knee down. They all vary.
