When I was thinking about this, I was wondering if it was too close to the original game Torment, where you wake up and you have no idea who you are and you’re going around the world trying to find out who you are. Is there already a game out there that’s so? Similar to this that maybe we should just not pursue this past the brainstorming phase? This isn’t something you necessarily need to talk about right away in brainstorming. But if you do decide after a brainstorming session that everybody’s really interested in this and you have a really good idea for a setting and a story and mechanics and art style and music style, maybe you need to double check that this hasn’t been done before. And the other thing that concerns me personally: I’m on record as saying I don’t like using amnesia, especially explosive amnesia.

Is this game too close to that? Is making you a clone of someone and therefore that person physically, but not mentally. Is that too close to just saying “Oh, this is just amnesia with a new hat on”? That’s something we probably want to discuss.

It’s certainly a concern, if I don’t like games based on Amnesia. Why? Am I making this game? Is this different? Is this different enough? Can we justify this?

These are things you should talk about at a brainstorming session. The reason I wanted to talk about this is I see people — you know I poo pooed ideas before and said: you know, ideas are nothing without implementation. But brainstorming sessions are really important. It’s a form of team bonding, but it’s also. You may have someone on your team who has a brilliant idea of how to do something — whether it’s a mechanic or a setting or something — and they just don’t know how to implement it because they haven’t thought of a good story for it or they haven’t thought of some way of bringing it to fruition. And that might come out in a brainstorming session and you might go: “This is brilliant, We’re going to do this, We’re going to make your game”. I’ve done a lot of brainstorming sessions. Not everybody is temperamentally suited to do brainstorming sessions, but I think running them the way I just described and that example I gave as how you would take a one-s sentence example of a game and tease it apart and all the different questions you should ask about it and how it should proceed and why you should have a bunch of different people at the brainstorming session, I think that’s a good example of, in general, how a brainstorming meeting should go.
