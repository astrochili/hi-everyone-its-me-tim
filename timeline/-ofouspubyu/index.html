A Christmas Poem By Ron Gilbert

2024-12-25

Game Industry

Watch on YouTube

Hi everyone. It’s me, Tim. Today I want to do a little Christmas poem by Ron Gilbert.

If you don’t know who Ron Gilbert is, he’s best known for creating scum, which was the script system that was used to create the adventure game Maniac mansion, and from there he went on to create a lot of other awesome adventure games like the secret of Monkey Island, Indiana Jones and the Last Crusade. He went on to do other games to like total Annihilation and death spank and the cave. So you may not know Ron Gilbert. You’ve probably played some of his games and he certainly has been really influential in the game industry.

Personally, I’ve never met him, at least I don’t think I’ve met him. I have a really bad memory for meeting people. He doesn’t appear in my notes anywhere, which is a strong indication that I haven’t met him. We seem to have a lot in common.

He’s had to deal with Publishers. He’s had to deal with not owning an IP that he’s credited for creating and watching other people do things in it. That’s not what this is about. A Christmas Carol — Christmas song that he rewrote the lyrics to be industry appropriate. He wrote it. He co-wrote it with Clayton ceric, who was a co-creator with him on death spank — death spank. They did this in December of 2004, which is 20 years ago.

He posted it I heard about it at some point. He posted it in 2021 and I will post a link below. I highly recommend you go to the site to read it, because he’s got an image for each one of the lines and then you don’t have to hear me say it, you can see it yourself. But I wanted to do it because a it’s Christmas, it’s the holidays, yay, I celebrate Christmas, and I want you to see how little has changed in 20 years, how Absolut, abolutely.

Dead on appropriate each line of this is. So let me, um, let me get ready. Okay, let me see if I can get this on here. Let me see, I don’t know, I’ll just do this, okay.

Tada and now let me read to you the 12 days of crunch time by Gilbert and ceric. On the 12th day of crunch time, my project gave to me 12 cents in royalties, 11 kiss ass previews, 10 nerdy testers, nine patent lawsuits, eight unplanned for features, seven frames, a second six angry spouses, five focus groups, four unstable hacks, three days without sleep, two sirly artists and a crappy publishing deal.

I can’t think of anything more appropriate to say to you on Christmas Day. I think I hope all of you have a Merry Christmas, a happy holiday, whatever you celebrate, just happy holiday everyone. Have a great day.
