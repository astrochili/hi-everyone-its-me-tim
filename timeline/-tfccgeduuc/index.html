Why I Don’t Solo Dev

2025-10-13

Production

Watch on YouTube

Hi everyone. It’s me, Tim. Today, I want to talk about why I don’t solo dev.

I’ve been asked this. I’ve been asked: hey, now that you’re semi-retired and you’re only working part-time, why don’t you make games all by yourself? Why don’t you solo dev?

You could do anything you want. Why not do that? So I decided to talk about why I don’t do it. Um, and I’m going to tell you, because there’s actually a bunch of reasons.

So I kind of ordered them. Eh, I, I put them in a particular order, Doesn’t matter what.

First, the probably one of the biggest reasons I don’t solo dev is: I know what I’m bad at And I’ve talked about this. I have a whole video, I think it’s called things I’m bad at. And I know what they are. I’ve done game development long enough that I know the things that I wasn’t good at that I got better at. And then there were some things I’m not good at that I simply am not going to. Get better at.

One of them is dialogue. I’ve talked about that. I I don’t write good NPC dialogue. I can write good descriptive text, I can write a monologue, but I’m not good at writing dialogues. I’m not good at, uh, polishing games. I can get a game to a good state, but I’ve always relied on other people who are really good at optimization and completing quests and also just getting everything that needs to be done to make to turn a game from an executable and a bunch of data on your machine to something that can be published. And this kind of leads into my second reason, which is there’s a lot that I don’t know about game development. And I know some of you are thinking, “But you’ve been doing this for so long”. And that’s kind of the reason I know I know so much about game development that I know what it is.

I don’t know. I’ve even talked about some of this. I mean, I’ve never done artwork. I’m really bad at artwork. I’ve tried getting some art into my games and it’s always been seen and removed.

I’ve never done music. I’ve written some song lyrics recently, but I haven’t made music. I’ve never even made a sound effect that’s made it into any of my games.

And I know what you’re thinking. You’re like: “Well, you’re, as a solo dev, you could buy all this stuff. You could go on to the various stores for Unity or Unreal and buy assets that people have made” And you’re right, I could. But I know, at the very minimum for art, that I’m so bad at judging it that even if I bought artwork, I probably would not do a good job of buying artwork.

That all made sense being in the same game. In other words, that were thematically similar or belonged in the same scene, or that the user interface I made would look correct for the game itself you’re playing. These are all things that I’ve watched artists do over decades. And I’m always impressed that they can look at a game and go: I know exactly what the inventory screen should look like, or I know exactly what the option screen should look like, or I have a good idea of what font we should use.

This is something I’m not good at and I know I’m not good at. So it’s not me saying I, I just wouldn’t want to do that. I don’t think I could do that.

And, by the way, if you’re a solo dev, you have to do all of this. You have to do all these things, Even those things you think you’re bad at, even if you’re buying assets. You have to be good at judging those assets And you may not be, but you got to do it anyway because you’re a solo dev. That’s the whole definition of being a solo developer. Third reason I don’t want to do solo dev is I love working in teams. I’ve got a video on that, about how much I love working on teams.

And I know some of you don’t. Some of you want to do solo dev because you don’t do that. You don’t think you work in teams well. But all I can say is this, A particular team might not be the best for you, but you will probably do your best in a team. There’s something about working with other people that is encouraging, that stokes passion, that gets you just moving in a way that you really don’t by yourself. You can really. You can only go so.

Much on your own inertia, but when you have other people working and they’re getting things done and that encourages you to get things done, that’s just super exciting in a way that’s hard to describe unless you’ve ever really worked on a good team. And I hope you do. I hope you get that experience.

Fourth reason I won’t do solo development is I would very, very much miss brainstorming. And I have a whole video about brainstorming where I describe what it’s like — especially when you first start making a new game and a new IP — to sit down with a group of people and brainstorm ideas. That’s what made me realize that some people have ideas that I never would have had in a million years. And even if we don’t use their idea directly as they presented it, there’s usually some germ of an idea in there that’s like “Oh, that’s. Brilliant, We should do this”.

In fact, when I make my own toys and I have a video — I’ll link it below — about my toys, and you can find a whole bunch of updates on toys and postmortems on my toys on this channel. But it’s the biggest thing I miss. When I’m here in my office at home and I’m making my toys, I really miss the fact that I don’t have someone to turn to and go: “Do you think I should do this?

Do you think this is a good way to go? Do you think I should do this instead? Do you have any ideas for what I should do here”. I don’t have anybody to do that with And, again, by definition, if I had somebody to do that with, I wouldn’t be doing solo dev. So it’s something that I already know.

If I started doing solo development, I would immediately almost run into that lack of brainstorming element and I wouldn’t like it I will tell you something that coding toys did make me love, which is the complete and utter lack of commitment. I don’t have anybody saying when are you going to be done with that? Or okay, this is good enough, we should ship it, because I don’t intend to ship any of my toys, I just like making them. The joy is in the making, but it’s not ever going to be in the publishing. So I’m not ever going to be a solo event.

And then the last thing I want to point, out — and I do have a video on this — every game I’ve ever made has been made better by someone else on the team. In other words, there’s been something — maybe it’s an idea of mine — that they improved. Maybe it’s an idea of mine. That got replaced by someone else’s better idea. But all I can tell you is every game I’ve ever worked on, there’s parts of it I can point to and go, “That was made better by someone else”. I have a video you can watch.

It was called my rejected ideas. I basically talk about things that I wanted in a game that got replaced with someone else on the team’s better idea. And I go through all those and I point out every single time it happened.

The result was a better system, mechanic, a better setting, uh detail, a better ending of a game. All of these were things that if I had been by myself, my original idea would have gone in there and it wouldn’t have been as good. And if you watch that video, you will see that I point out a lot of things that other people did that elevated. The game I was making And that kind of made me over the years go, “Wow, if I had done solo dev, all of those elements of that game would have been of. Those games would have been worse than what shipped”. So in that sense this isn’t a hypothetical of I don’t think I’d like solo dev. I have proof that solo dev wouldn’t be as good for me as team development.

Now I’ve said all this. I’ve said there are like five big reasons there. I’m saying all this.

I’m not trying to discourage you from doing solo dev if you want to do it. In fact, I often want to encourage people. Do solo dev. You will learn so much about game development even if you end up going into team development.

But what I am trying to tell you is you should try to do more than what I’ve done. You should try to be better than I was. I mean, isn’t that the whole point of this? Isn’t that the point of any teacher or parent or whatever mentor, whatever role you are? When you’re trying to get younger people to do something, isn’t the ultimate goal to have them be better at it than what you were? And if that’s the case, you know I encourage you to solo dev. You may be awesome at it and I may get my wish, which is I may get a really awesome game that I get to play. So when I’m in retirement, there will be a source of games, whether it’s from people doing team development or people doing solo development. But I can just tell you, solo de solo development it’s not for me.
