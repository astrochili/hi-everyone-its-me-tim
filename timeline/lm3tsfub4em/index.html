So what did I learn from all these things? I’ I’ve said it as I’m going along, but I’ll tell you the three big takeaways I have from having other things that I do affect my game: designs. One: planning starts early. You want to make sure you have the things you need. You don’t want a half mixed bowl that you’re of something you’re going to bake or something that’s half cooked on the skillet, and then you discover you’re out of salt. Then you discover you don’t have any butter or oil or the right oil and you’re like: well, what can you do? The bowl is half mixed and maybe the wet has already hit the dry and now clock’s ticking on when this thing has to go in the oven. Or maybe it’s half cooked in the skillet and it’s like: well, you’re going to have to finish cooking it, you’re going to have to figure out how to substitute.

Not good. What you really should do is put out all your ingredients before you ever start making whatever you’re making. Another thing is: you got to. Keep an eye on things and at the right times. You can ignore it for a while, but then you have to re keep an eagle eye on it at the end.

Uh, making a game is the same way. You have to pay attention to the right things at the right time as you go along. And sometimes it’s not an eye. Sometimes you listen to things, maybe it’s what people were saying about the game, maybe it’s. You’re really far into the game and you’re realizing your mechanic’s not working because there’s no good visual and audio trigger. You need them both, maybe you just go by gut. Um, I cook a lot and bake a lot just by feels. Sometimes I design by feels this function, this design mechanic.

It’s. In as described, but it’s not feeling right, so sometimes you just have to change it. And then, finally, the thing I really learned: it takes a long time to learn a new skill.

You don’t become a good Baker overnight. You don’t watch people bake on TV and then become a good Baker.

It takes time, it takes practice and you’re going to mess up, and that’s okay. You’re also going to learn what your limitations are. I’m a better Baker, then I’m a cook. I can write short form — uh writing — but long form writing, especially dialogue, has eluded me. I’ve learned to accept that limitation. You’ll have to learn the same thing. So these are the big three things that I’ve learned from my hobbies and other things: planning, keeping an eye on it and just taking the time to do it. So, Ezra, I hope that answered your question.
