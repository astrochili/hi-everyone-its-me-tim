So I was thinking here that maybe, just maybe, if combat skills had more utility outside of combat, that might encourage some players to try the non-combat solutions. Thinking well, I spent points on these combat skills, but hey, I can use them outside of combat too. They have utility, maybe I should do that instead. And they always know they can fall back on just combat if they wanted. So let’s run down some ideas I had of how to use combat skills outside of combat. One thing that could be used in dialogue more than I think has been done before is ways to modify the player being intimidating. I could see you having a speech and unarmed combination and say, “Hey, unless you me want you, unless you want me to knock you unconscious right here and now, you tell your boss I’m here to see him”. That could be really fun. It could be a great way of making your character more intimidating, because he’s got really high combat skills. And also pointing out: here are the combat skills I’m using. That’s great. I also thought that there could be more synergy between basic player attributes and skills. I know this is kind of weird, because usually attributes are used to form the basis of skills so that you know if you have a high, you know decks, you have a high dodge. What if, whenever you made ammo or mods for particular weapons, you got some kind of bonus if your skill in that weapon was higher? If you’re making ammo, maybe you make more ammo. I think I think that’s enough. Let me just tell you a quick conclusion here. I think any non-combat use of a combat skill is good in the sense it will make the player feel like the purchase of that combat skill was worth it, even if it then leads to them getting into combat less rarely or more rarely — which all the talk I hear about RPGs making players into murder hobos, the idea of them spending points in a combat skill and then doing less combat because there are more solutions to getting around you know a or doing a quest without using combat, That seems like a win to me and it certainly seems like something that, uh, people should dive into.
